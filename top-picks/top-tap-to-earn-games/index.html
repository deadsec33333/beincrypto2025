Best Telegram Tap-to-Earn Games to Play in 2025

By Sofya Odintsova
31 July 2024, 14:47 GMT+0000
Updated by Dmitriy Maiorov
4 April 2025, 13:31 GMT+0000
Tap-to-earn games allow users to get real tokens by simple clicks. These only appeared in 2024 but since then have gained popularity, attracting millions of users. The main reason is the simplicity... of mechanics–you do not need special knowledge or any kind of equipment but a smartphone to earn money.

Telegram has become the main platform for tap-to-earn games due to its global reach and ease of integrating crypto-based services. Crypto trading bots and apps are available right within the messenger, which makes digital assets accessible for a wide audience.

Key benefits of using Telegram-based trading bots and apps include:
● Accessibility and convenience
● Low entry barrier
● Crypto earning potential
● Community hype and momentum

TonTrader is a gamified cryptocurrency platform in the Telegram ecosystem. Unlike traditional exchanges, TonTrader eliminates KYC, deposits and asset transfers, offering instant trading access right inside the popular messenger. It is integrated with The Open Network (TON) via Telegram Wallet. 

The heart of the project is its native token, DOLLR. Users can earn the coin without initial investments by clicking a button every eight hours and inviting friends. Another way to get DOLLR is through performing special tasks like watching educational content. 

The TonTrader’s key feature is Trading Arena, where users compete in tournaments by paying an entry fee in DOLLR. The whole process is simple: you just need to trade and achieve maximum profitability. Top traders win real rewards from the USDT prize pool.

ZAVOD is a clicker game, integrated into the MDAO Telegram Wallet. It is a part of MarsDAO Web3 ecosystem, which creates educational tools, infrastructure solutions and other earning apps. 

In ZAVOD, users begin as workers with basic equipment and earn ZP by completing tasks that simulate production processes. Players can invest ZP to upgrade workbenches and tools, boosting their hourly ZP income. Tool upgrades alter collection frequency: higher levels mean less frequent but likely larger hauls, creating a strategic trade-off.

Additional features include joining unions for bonuses, a referral program offering 20% of invited friends’ ZP and daily missions for extra ZP.

ZAVOD’s players also can earn USDT. For that, they need to get MWD coins, a TRC-20 meme token created by MarsDAO. Active players and those owning Level 2 Golden Helmets can receive MWD tokens as rewards. The game’s team distributes USDT on Astra Wallets every two weeks.

OKX Racer is a mini-game developed by the cryptocurrency exchange OKX, available within the Telegram messaging platform. The game centers around predicting Bitcoin price movements, offering users a simplified way to engage with crypto market dynamics.

Players participate by forecasting whether the price of Bitcoin will rise or fall. Successful predictions accumulate points, with consecutive correct guesses providing progressively higher rewards through a multiplier system. The game also incorporates special timed rounds where players can earn additional points for accurate short-term predictions.

Additionally, users can receive rewards for completing different tasks, such as connecting TON with OKX Wallet or verifying on OKX. There can also be tasks connected with OKX Racer’s partnership ecosystems. The project has teamed up with many Telegram mini-apps, including Tomarket, CATS, DOGS and Catizen. 

As an official OKX product integrated with the TON ecosystem, the game represents one of the first instances of a major cryptocurrency exchange developing this type of lightweight gaming experience. Since its launch, OKX Racer has attracted a substantial user base. At its peak, the game had over 10 million monthly active participants.

Hamster Kombat is a Telegram-based clicker game operating on TON. Launched in March 2024 amid the Notcoin hype, it quickly gained over 300 million users across 190 countries. 

During the first season, users assumed the role of a cryptocurrency exchange CEO, managing a virtual platform (such as Binance or HTX). The core gameplay involved tapping the screen to earn in-game coins, with accumulated tokens determining the player’s level. 

On September 26, 2024, Hamster Kombat launched its HMSTR token with a total supply of 100 billion coins. The token was listed on major exchanges, including OKX, Binance and Bybit, followed by one of the largest airdrops in crypto history—distributed to 131 million players. However, the average reward per user was modest (around $8), and the token’s subsequent price decline further reduced its value.

On February 25, 2025, the team announced Hamster Network, a TON-based Layer 2 blockchain designed for Web3 applications. Built using TON Virtual Machine, it ensures full smart contract compatibility with the mainnet while improving scalability. 

In the second season of Hamster Kombat, players will build and upgrade their own gaming studios. The exact date of its launch remains unclear though.

Notcoin operates through a Telegram bot, blending GameFi elements and play-to-earn (P2E) opportunities. It is built on TON and combines addictive gameplay with real token rewards. At its peak, Notcoin had over 35 million monthly active users. 

The project employs a distinctive consensus mechanism that blends Proof-of-Activity (PoA) and Proof-of-Interaction (PoI). This hybrid model ensures that users earn the in-game NOT tokens for active participation (like clicking and completing tasks) and social engagement (such as inviting friends or joining group activities). 

Notcoin launched its native token NOT in May, 2024. The coin got listed on such major exchanges like Binance, OKX, Bybit, etc. As of the time of writing, its market capitalization stands at almost $280 million. 

NOT is a deflationary asset. The project’s team periodically conducts strategic reductions of the total token supply through a burn mechanism. This approach creates artificial scarcity–when combined with stable or growing demand, it may positively impact the asset’s long-term valuation.

Notcoin is actively expanding. Recently, it has presented a Not Games, which is going to become a platform with multiple games united by the NOT token. Now the project’s ecosystem includes such games as Not Pixel and Lost Dogs.

Developed by Pixelverse, PixelTap is a Web3 clicker game that combines simple tap mechanics with player-versus-player (PvP) battles within Telegram. As part of Pixelverse’s cyberpunk gaming ecosystem, the game rewards players with PIXFI tokens for active participation.

Players tap the screen to earn in-game coins, which can be used to purchase and upgrade battle bots—AI-powered companions that fight in PvP matches. What is Tap-to-Earn

Tap-to-earn is a subcategory of play-to-earn (P2E) within the broader GameFi sector. They usually run as mini-apps within Telegram. The core mechanic of these clicker games revolves around tapping the screen. The main goal is to accumulate as much in-game currency as possible to upgrade tapping power, participate in tournaments, or exchange earned rewards for real tokens.

Clicker games gained popularity relatively recently—only in 2024. Tap-to-earn apps continue to attract millions of users. Their success lies in their simplicity and potential to generate real income.

How Do Tap-to-Earn Games Work

As their name implies, tapping is the core mechanic of tap-to-earn games. Users just click the screen repeatedly or at specific intervals. For instance, they might click a button, a character, or an object to “mine” or “collect” in-game resources to earn a crypto award. 

Some games add light variations, like tapping to complete mini-tasks (e.g., “harvest crops” or “hit a target”).

Each tap (or set of taps) earns a small amount of in-game currency or points. In crypto-based games, these might be tied to blockchain tokens that can later be traded or cashed out. Rewards often scale with time, upgrades or player effort. Tapping more frequently or consistently might yield bonuses.

To keep players engaged, these games usually include a progression system. Users can spend their earned currency to buy upgrades—like faster earning rates, auto-tappers (bots that tap for you), or multipliers—that reduce the need for constant manual tapping. Some games introduce levels or milestones, unlocking new features or higher rewards as you advance.

Many tap-to-earn games impose limits to prevent endless tapping, like an “energy” bar that depletes with each tap and refills over time or with in-game purchases. This encourages periodic check-ins rather than constant play.

How to Choose a Tap-to-Earn Game

Choosing a tap-to-earn game requires balancing potential rewards with risks, legitimacy and personal enjoyment. First, you need to decide if you are playing for entertainment or to earn crypto rewards. If it is the latter, focus on games with tangible payouts (e.g., crypto or gift cards) rather than just in-game points.

Also, consider how much time you are willing to invest. Some games demand frequent check-ins, while others reward passive play. Waiting for the token launch and airdrop, which can take many months, is another time-consuming point. 

Look for games from known companies or teams with a track record. Check their website, social media or X posts for transparency and activity. Avoid games from anonymous or newly created entities. Pay attention to community feedback and do not forget to check if the chosen project has a roadmap, which would mean that the developers aim at long-term utility. 

Risks Associated with Tap-to-Earn Games

While clicker games can be fun and potentially profitable, players should be aware of the following potential downsides:

  • Low or No Real Earnings: Many games advertise big rewards, but the actual payout might be negligible after accounting for time spent. Token values can be volatile or crash, leaving earnings worthless, like it happened to Hamster Kombat. 
  • Scams and Rug Pulls: Some tap-to-earn games are outright scams. Developers might hype the game, collect user investments or ad revenue, and disappear without paying out rewards.
  • Hidden Costs: While most are free-to-play, in-app purchases or “boosters” can tempt players to spend real money for marginal gains. 
  • Bugs or Glitches: Poorly designed games might fail to track progress or deliver promised rewards, leaving players empty-handed.

Conclusion 

Tap-to-earn games on Telegram, like Notcoin, Hamster Kombat, and Catizen, became a real phenomenon in 2024. Such clicker games will remain popular in 2025 and probably will not disappear beyond that. They still attract millions of users worldwide due to their simplicity and potential for passive income. 

However, to get a real crypto award for playing tap-to-earn games on Telegram, users should evaluate some associated risks. These might include low earnings, rug pulls, hidden costs, and different bugs.
